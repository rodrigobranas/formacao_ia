# ADR-001: Global Header Toggle with Instant Unit Conversion

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2025-06-25

## Context

The weather dashboard currently displays all measurements in Celsius and metric units (km/h, mm, hPa). The original climate panel PRD explicitly deferred °C/°F switching, but the product vision (`PRODUCT.md`, `DESIGN.md`) and design reference (`docs/design/index.html`) already specify a segmented °C/°F control in the sticky header. Learners and users expect consumer-weather conventions: one global unit preference, instant feedback, and persistence across sessions.

Stakeholders confirmed scope includes temperature plus wind, precipitation, and pressure—not dual-unit display or per-metric toggles. Default remains Celsius; preference must survive page reloads; switching must update visible numbers immediately without a loading state.

## Decision

Adopt a **single global °C/°F segmented toggle in the header** that:

1. Converts **all in-scope measurements instantly** across the dashboard when toggled (temperature, wind, precipitation, pressure).
2. **Defaults to Celsius** on first visit.
3. **Persists the user's choice** across browser sessions.
4. Keeps **one active unit system at a time**—no simultaneous dual labels.

Presentation follows the existing design spec: glass segmented control in the top bar, keyboard-operable, with clear active/inactive states.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Phased delivery (temperature first, other metrics later)

- **Description**: Ship header toggle affecting only temperature in MVP; extend to wind, precipitation, and pressure in a follow-up release.
- **Pros**: Smaller initial scope; faster first delivery.
- **Cons**: Incomplete experience—users toggling to Fahrenheit would still see km/h and mm, breaking the "global units" mental model.
- **Why rejected**: Stakeholder chose full metric conversion in one release; partial conversion would confuse users and undermine the toggle's purpose.

### Alternative 2: Locale-aware default with global toggle

- **Description**: Detect browser/system locale on first visit to set Celsius or Fahrenheit automatically, with persistence and instant conversion of all metrics.
- **Pros**: Aligns with platform conventions (e.g., US users see °F first); matches best practices from consumer weather apps.
- **Cons**: Adds first-visit complexity; conflicts with explicit stakeholder choice to keep **Celsius as default** for this training sandbox.
- **Why rejected**: Stakeholder confirmed Celsius default; locale detection deferred to a future phase.

### Alternative 3: Dual-unit display mode

- **Description**: Show primary and secondary units together (e.g., `21°C (70°F)`).
- **Pros**: Helps users who think in both systems; no information loss when switching.
- **Cons**: Clutters the data-first UI; increases cognitive load on hero and hourly curve; out of scope for MVP.
- **Why rejected**: Stakeholder explicitly excluded dual display and separate per-metric toggles from this effort.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Delivers a long-deferred capability aligned with product vision and design reference.
- Matches familiar consumer-weather interaction patterns (header toggle, instant update, remembered preference).
- Gives learners a complete example of preference state, persistence, and consistent formatting across a dashboard.

### Negative

- All visible surfaces that show converted measurements must stay in sync when the toggle changes—broader UI touch surface than temperature-only.
- Rounding and label consistency across cards become a product quality concern (values must not "jump" inconsistently between sections).

### Risks

- **Incomplete coverage**: A card or label left in the old unit undermines trust. Mitigation: define an explicit inventory of in-scope readings in the PRD and validate each during QA.
- **Accessibility regression**: Toggle must remain keyboard-operable with clear selected state. Mitigation: follow `DESIGN.md` segmented control spec and WCAG AA contrast on active segment.
